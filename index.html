Figure 1: Block diagrams of (a) the proposed MTL-Tacotron, that is presented in Section III, and (b) a contrastive model, PE-Tacotron, that is discussed in Section IV. Both employ a prosody generator to explicitly model prosodic phrasing. The prosody generator produces prosody embedding (PE) from input text, that forms a joint embedding vector with character embedding. Blue color indicates those involved in training, while pink color highlights a pre-trained module. Griffin-Lim algorithm is not involved in training.



Tacotron: Tacotron model
PE-Tacotron: PE and input character embeddings (CE_i) are added together and the PE are extracted by pretrained prosody generater.
MTL-Tacotron: PE and output character embeddings (CE_o) are added together and the PE are extracted by prosody generater (secondary task) which is jointly training with the Tacotron model (main task).
